In \citep{sakurai2014modern} \texteqnref 6.16 is

\begin{equation}\label{eqn:squaredAngularMomentumExpansion:20}
\BL^2 = \Bx^2 \Bp^2 - \lr{ \Bx \cdot \Bp }^2 + i \Hbar \Bx \cdot \Bp,
\end{equation}

and a derivation of the same.  The derivation is clear right until the end, where the details for the last steps are left off.  When I attempted those last steps I got a different sign for the \( \Bx \cdot \Bp \) term.  I also get that same difference in sign if I do this expansion myself:

\begin{dmath}\label{eqn:squaredAngularMomentumExpansion:40}
\BL^2
=
\epsilon_{i j k} x_i p_j \epsilon_{r s k} x_r p_s
=
\delta_{i j}^{[r s]}
x_i p_j x_r p_s
=
x_i p_j x_i p_j - x_i p_j x_j p_i
=
x_i \lr{ x_i p_j -i \Hbar \delta_{i j}} p_j - x_i p_j \lr{ p_i x_j + i \Hbar \delta_{i j} }
=
\Bx^2 \Bp^2 - i \Hbar \Bx \cdot \Bp - x_i p_j p_i x_j - i \Hbar \Bx \cdot \Bp
=
\Bx^2 \Bp^2 - 2 i \Hbar \Bx \cdot \Bp - x_i p_i p_j x_j
=
\Bx^2 \Bp^2 - 2 i \Hbar \Bx \cdot \Bp - (\Bx \cdot \Bp) \lr{ x_j p_j - i \Hbar }
=
\Bx^2 \Bp^2 - i \Hbar \Bx \cdot \Bp - (\Bx \cdot \Bp)^2.
\end{dmath}

I couldn't spot an error anywhere above, but Prof Paramekanti spotted it when I asked.  The error is in the second last step above, since \( p_j x_j = p_1 x_1 + p_2 x_2 + p_3 x_3 \) and flipping the order must be done to each.

\begin{dmath}\label{eqn:squaredAngularMomentumExpansion:60}
\BL^2
=
\Bx^2 \Bp^2 - 2 i \Hbar \Bx \cdot \Bp - (\Bx \cdot \Bp) \lr{ x_j p_j - 3 i \Hbar }
=
\Bx^2 \Bp^2 + i \Hbar \Bx \cdot \Bp - (\Bx \cdot \Bp)^2.
\end{dmath}

The text is correct.
